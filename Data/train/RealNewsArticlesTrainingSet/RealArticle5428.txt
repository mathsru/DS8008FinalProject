James Corden on Barcelona: We can't let terror attacks become accepted as 'normal' James Corden honored the victims of Thursday’s horrific terror attack in Barcelona and stressed the importance of not allowing these attacks to be accepted as the norm.

The late-night host, 38, began his show Thursday by sharing a solemn message in the aftermath of the attack, which has been claimed by ISIS and left at least 14 people dead.

“When we hear about (terror attacks in) London or Paris, Stockholm and now Barcelona, you could almost start to become numb to it, like this is something becoming normal and we don’t need to talk about it here on our show,” Corden said. “But we think we mustn’t allow it to become something that’s normal. We must talk about it so that we remember how sad, horrific and tragic these moments are.

“To the people of Spain and Barcelona, I just want to add to the rest of the world outpouring of love and give our thoughts for you.”

James Corden addressed the terror attack in Barcelona with an emotional message on his show.

The terror attack occurred when the driver of a rental van charged through a busy street in the Spanish city that was packed with pedestrians. Dozens of people were injured, in addition to the deaths.

The harrowing act of terror was carried out hours after an explosion took place in a house roughly 60 miles away in an incident law enforcement officials say was related. Four people have been arrested in connection to the attacks.

Corden ended his powerful message Thursday evening by paying tribute to the victims.

“We are thinking about the victims and their families on what must be unimaginable day of horror for them,” he said.

Fellow CBS late-night host Stephen Colbert shared a similar message on his program Thursday evening.

“This is heartbreaking reminder that evil is real, and that the United States is not alone in fighting it,” Colbert said after sending his thoughts to Barcelona.
