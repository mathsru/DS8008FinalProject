Melyssa Ford suffers skull fracture, concussion after 'horrific' car crash Melyssa Ford is on the mend after a near-fatal car accident in Los Angeles left her with serious head injuries Thursday.

The former reality TV star and model, 41, suffered a skull fracture, concussion and bleeding in her brain after an 18-wheeler clipped her Jeep Wrangler on the Ventura Freeway, a rep for the hip-hop model told Us Weekly.

The “Blood, Sweat & Heels” star’s vehicle was sent into a “tailspin” and flipped three times before landing upside down after the truck merged into her lane and clipped her back right tire as she headed to friend Zena Foster’s bridal shower.

“Real Housewives of Atlanta” star Claudia Jordan shared a shocking image of her friend’s accident that featured Ford’s Jeep upside down and Ford receiving help from a trio of Good Samaritans.

“She was involved in a horrific accident that could have easily taken her life. It’s actually a miracle she survived. She’s injured FOR REAL,” Jordan wrote, noting that she’d gotten Ford’s permission to post the picture.

The reality star also called on any witnesses to come forward with information that might help Ford put “the pieces back together,” as she does not remember much of the crash.

Ford’s rep said a 10-inch open wound was stitched back together in surgery, and that she also has multiple gashes on her arms and legs.

She is reportedly recovering in a Los Angeles hospital.

Ford rose to fame as a music video model, and has appeared in clips for hit songs like “Yeah!” by Usher and “Big Pimpin’” by JAY-Z. She was previously a radio personality for SiriusXM’s Hot Jamz channel, and in 2014, appeared on the Bravo reality series “Blood, Sweat & Heels.”

She’s also appeared in several films, including the 2012 Kevin Hart comedy “Think Like a Man” and its 2014 sequel.
