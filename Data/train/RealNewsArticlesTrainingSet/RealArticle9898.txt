Rafael Nadal stopped by security guard who didn't recognize him at Paris Masters Rafael Nadal, the top-ranked tennis player in the world, was denied entry onto a court at the Paris Masters tournament.

In the cringe-worthy video posted by Sport Reporter, Nadal had to tell a security guard who he was before the over-zealous court bouncer would let him through to the grounds.

Rafael Nadal, the number one ranked tennis player in the world, was denied entry onto a court at the Paris Masters tournament.

First, the guard asks him if he has a pass to enter, to which Nadal responds that he does not. The security official then asks him “Who are you?” and the ATP No. 1-ranked star replies, simply “Rafael Nadal.”

“Nadal, OK, thank you,” the guard says as he lets him pass.

Nadal seemed to take the guard’s confusion in stride as he gave him a quick pat on the arm before finally gaining admission to the court.

Unfortunately for Nadal, he had to pull out of the tournament on Friday, citing a right knee injury. The knee was taped in his third-round win over Pablo Cuevas but was the apparent reason he chose not to face his next opponent, Serbian qualifier Filip Krajinovic.

“I have to pull out,” Nadal later said to the press. “Of course it’s a very tough decision for me, especially here in Paris, which has been the most important city in my career without a doubt.”

The Spaniard is a 10-time French Open Champion. His status for the ATP finals is unclear.
