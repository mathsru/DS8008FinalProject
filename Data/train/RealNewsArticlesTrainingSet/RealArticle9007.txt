Ashton Kutcher is relieved that ABC's "Bachelor in Paradise" will be returning this summer.

"Oh man, oh man, I'm so happy 'Paradise' is back," the actor said on ABC's "GMA" on Wednesday.

Over the past week, the "Bachelor" spinoff has been enveloped in scandal over alleged sexual misconduct while taping a scene in which DeMario Jackson reportedly took advantage of Corinne Olympios while she was too intoxicated to consent.

On Tuesday, Warner Bros., which produces "Bachelor in Paradise," announced that its investigation found no proof of misconduct in the incident. The production is set to resume on season four with a week, according to reports.

"I'm telling you I was worried, I was concerned," Kutcher continued as he put his hand over his chest as if he was feeling anxious.
