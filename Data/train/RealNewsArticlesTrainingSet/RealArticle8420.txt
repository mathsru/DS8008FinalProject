Why Hilary Swank Took Three Years Off from Hollywood Hilary Swank is circling her way back into Hollywood after taking three years off in order to care for her ailing father. In an interview with Stephen Colbert Tuesday, the two-time Oscar winner updated fans on her father’s health, sharing that he’s doing well after undergoing a lung transplant.

“He’s great, thank you for asking,” she told the late-night host. “It’s the hardest surgery a person can undergo, and I thank all the people who make themselves a donor, it saves lives.”

Swank explained that doctors had “given him a short time to live” if he didn’t get the surgery. Now, though, “he’s alive and thriving and well. I took three years off to be with him and help him through that life journey.”

Swank first revealed the news about her father’s condition back in 2015, announcing her acting hiatus shortly after his surgery.

“My dad is living with me,” she told HuffPost Live. “I’m his sole caretaker now.”

Though there were certain job opportunities that she had to pass on, making the choice to care for her father was an easy one, said Swank. “There is nothing I want to do more other than being with my dad in this time of need. And if it wasn’t me taking care of him, I think I would always look back and regret that opportunity to be able to take care of him and help him through this extraordinary time.” And even when she was taking time away from the screen, Swank kept busy; she launched an athleisure line called Mission Statement in the fall of 2016.
