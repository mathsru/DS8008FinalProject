Kirstie Ennis, a snowboarder and mountaineer, will also appear as the Body Issue's first-ever featured veteran, and Novlene Williams-Mills, a four-time Olympic track and field medalist from Jamaica, will be the first breast cancer survivor featured in the issue.

The yearly issue celebrates athletic achievement and highlights the grace and beauty inherent in the strength of world-class athletes. In the video from her shoot, Wozniacki talks about learning to get over her insecurities about her muscles and how she stopped comparing herself to other women who aren't professional athletes. "I grew up to really love my body and the strength that I have," she said. "Now I have a strong core, I have abs, I have a butt and I have thighs, because that's what I need to run fast and be able to sustain the power out there when I play against the other women." The tennis pro posted her cover on Instagram, writing, "Proud and excited for this one! Thank you #ESPNBody for the cover!!"

Elliott and Báez also posted pictures of their covers on Instagram, and Waterson and Ennis uploaded behind-the-scenes images from the shoot. "Remember when Barbie created that cute doll with a little more curves? 5' short little thing packing a bit more in the upper and lower halves? Hi, here I am, an example. Curves and all, one leg," Ennis captioned her post. "I love this body and the hell I've put it through. Thank God, I have been so blessed. I love imperfection, and I love 'imperfect' bodies — they all tell stories."
