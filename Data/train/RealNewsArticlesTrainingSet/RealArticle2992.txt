"I did have a healing of cancer": Val Kilmer FINALLY admits to battling disease "I did have a healing of cancer": Val Kilmer FINALLY admits to battling disease

The 57 year-old made the admission in a Reddit chat

Val Kilmer attends the 23rd Annual Simply Shakespeare Benefit Reading Of "The Two Gentlemen Of Verona" at The Broad Stage on September 25, 2013 in Santa Monica, California. (Image: WireImage)

He recently denied having throat cancer after Michael Douglas said he was battling the condition.

‌



But now it seems that Hollywood star Val Kilmer has admitted that he has fought the disease. It comes after the 57 year-old took part in a Reddit Q&A called Ask Me Anything, last week.

‌



One user enquired: "A while ago, Michael Douglas claimed you had terminal cancer. What was the story behind that?"

‌



Actor Val Kilmer seems to have confirmed he was suffering with cancer (Image: Getty)

Responding, Val said: "He was probably trying to help me cause press probably asked where I was these days, and I did have a healing of cancer, but my tongue is still swollen altho healing all the time (sic).

"Because I don’t sound my normal self yet people think I may still be under the weather."

‌



His turn of phrase - "a healing of cancer" - is likely related to Scientology, of which he's a subscriber. The church believes that poor health can be healed through prayer because it's essentially a mental error.

The Mirror have contacted a representative for Val Kilmer, but are yet to receive a response.

The actor, 57, has been the subject of rumours for some time (Image: Instagram/valkilmerofficial) Kilmer is shown in the movie Batman Forever

His apparent admission comes just months after he denied having any form of cancer.

In a lengthy post to Facebook which surfaced after fellow star Michael Douglas implied he'd been diagnosed, he said: "I love Michael Douglas but he is misinformed.

"The last time I spoke to him was almost two years ago, when I asked him for a referral for a specialist to get a diagnosis for a lump in my throat, which prevented me from continuing a tour of my play CITIZEN TWAIN."

‌



"I ended up using a team at UCLA and have no cancer whatsoever. I still have a swollen tongue and am rehabbing steadily."
