The Golden Globe Ambassador: Presenting Hollywood's Next Generation Chosen by the HFPA, the Golden Globe Ambassador – previously known as Miss or Mr. Golden Globe – is traditionally the daughter or son of one of the industry’s most respected actors/actresses/directors/producers and assists during the Golden Globe Awards ceremony. The tradition started in 1962 and over the years has introduced many second and third-generation stars; among them: Linda Evans, Anne Archer, Laura Dern, Melanie Griffith, and her daughter, Dakota Johnson. In 2017 the title has been changed to Golden Globe Ambassador to better reflect the role and express the inclusiveness that has always been central to the Hollywood Foreign Press Association’s mandate.
