Céline Dion Can't Help but Sing Along to Cher's Billboard Music Awards Performance "My Heart Will Go On" wasn't the only song Céline Dion sang at the 2017 Billboard Music Awards.

The Canadian powerhouse took the stage to perform a breathtaking, chills-inducing rendition of the Titanic theme song in honor of the film's twentieth anniversary. But shortly after Dion left the stage, she couldn't help but burst into song once more—this time she was singing along to her icon Cher's performance of "Believe."

Dion was stopped dead in her tracks backstage by hearing Cher—who slayed the BBMA stage by belting out her 1998 dance-pop hit "Believe" wearing nothing but heart-shaped pasties, a thong, and a "robe" made out of strands of silver jewels (further proving her Icon award was well-deserved). And like all of us, Dion couldn't help but dance and sing along with the 71-year-old, leading the press room in an impromptu karaoke session of the song that rocked radio waves nearly two decades ago.
