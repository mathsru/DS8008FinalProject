More 'Jersey Shore'? 'MTV Floribama Shore' set for debut Nov. 27, 2017, 6:25 p.m. ET

It’s “Jersey Shore” — the next chapter.

“MTV Floribama Shore,” billed as a return of the network’s “Shore” franchise, debuts 10 p.m. Monday, Nov. 27 on MTV.

“Different shore, same shenanigans,” says MTV. “Get ready for all the drama, mayhem, and hookups, brought to you from the party capital that is Panama City Beach.”

On the boarder of Florida and Alabama.

“It’s about to go down,” says castmember Candace Rice in a teaser that also features plenty of flesh, fights and hook-ups, not unlike the original “Jersey Shore.”

SallyAnn Salsano, the creator and executive producer of “Jersey Shore,” is at the helm of “MTV Floribama Shore.”

Yet, cast members say Panama City Beach is quite different than Seaside Heights.

“They fist pump, we dab!” says cast member Kirk Medas to US Weekly.

“It’s a completely different part of the country. Obviously we’re going to be different. We’re not from Jersey,” says Gus Smyrnios to US. “Every part of country, people are different. We’re trying to show how people are on the Floribama side, the Southeast region.”

Instead of GTL, or the “Gym, Tan and Laundry” of the Seaside Heights crew, it’s Triple “B,” “Bench, Babes, and Bars” for Floribama.

Most of the cast of “Jersey Shore,” including Mike “The Situation” Sorrentino and Nicole “Snooki” Polizzi, reunited on “Reunion Road Trip: Return to the Jersey Shore,” which aired August 20 on E! They also filmed a Burger King commercial at the BK in Barnegat in July.

MTV filmed a new version of its “MTV Beach House” in June at a private residence in Loveladies and at Bayview Park in Long Beach Township on Long Beach Island.
