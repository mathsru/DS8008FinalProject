Hannibal Buress Charges Dropped After Miami Arrest Charges have been dropped against Hannibal Buress after he was arrested for disorderly intoxication in Miami on December 10, People reports. “We are extremely pleased that the prosecutors took a careful look at the facts and listened to what we presented to them and agreed to dismiss the case in its entirety,” Buress’ attorney said in a statement. “In Florida, it’s protected First Amendment speech to ask a police officer to call you an Uber. It’s certainly not a crime and when coming from a professional comedian it’s actually quite funny. Unfortunately for Hannibal, the arresting officers didn’t think so.”

Following the arrest, footage of the incident surfaced online. Buress was shown handcuffed, yelling at an officer and refusing to get into a police car. “What am I being detained for,” he asked two police officers. One officer said “trespassing” but later was heard saying “disorderly intoxication.” Buress responded, “No, I'm not getting in your car unless you have probable cause for anything. ... Take the handcuffs off of me, I didn’t do anything, I’m not getting in your car.”
