Kate Hudson pokes fun at Scaramucci with ‘How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days’ meme Ousted White House communications director Anthony Scaramucci’s short D.C. tenure looks all too familiar to Kate Hudson.

The actress got in on the meme fun after Scaramucci left his position Monday after 11 days in office with a funny Instagram post echoing her 2003 romantic comedy “How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days.”

“Box office results are in! The #1 comedy in America!” she wrote, alongside a photo featuring Trump and Scaramucci posing next to the original film’s title.

Scaramucci lasted less than two weeks in his new position, but left quite a legacy, thanks to a now-infamous, expletive-laced rant against several Trump staffers, including former chief of staff Reince Priebus.

“How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days,” meanwhile, starred Hudson and Matthew McConaughey as two scheming singletons who both make bets unbeknownst to the other — hers, that she can get a man to dump her within 10 days, and his, that he can make a woman fall in love with him in the same time period.

And though Hudson’s film was billed as a romantic comedy, Scaramucci’s time in the White House was anything but romantic — his wife filed for divorce just before the birth of their son, claiming her husband was obsessed with getting Trump’s approval.

Hudson wasn’t the only one to joke about his exit.

Many took the “You’re Fired!” route after Trump’s infamous “The Apprentice” catchphrase, while others took the opportunity to poke fun at fellow Trump administration victims Sean Spicer and Reince Priebus.
