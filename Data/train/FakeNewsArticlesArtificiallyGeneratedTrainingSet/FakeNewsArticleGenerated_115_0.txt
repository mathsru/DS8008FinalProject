A Battambang Man Arrested for Drug Possession and Smoking

Battambang, Battambang Province, Cambodia - A local resident, Davis Story, was arrested by the Battambang police on Wednesday for drug possession and smoking in public.

According to the police report, Davis Story, a 35-year-old man, was arrested at around 11:00 pm while he was driving his car on the national road 10. The police were patrolling the area when they noticed Story smoking in his car and acting suspiciously.

When the police approached Story, they found a small bag of drugs, believed to be marijuana, hidden in his car. Story allegedly admitted to possessing the drugs and claimed that he was planning to sell them to a friend.

The police arrested Story and took him to the Battambang police station for questioning. He was later charged with drug possession and smoking in public.

In an interview with the Battambang Times, Judge Prak Sok, who is handling the case, said that the law is clear and Story will face severe consequences if he is found guilty.

"This is a serious offense and Story must be held accountable for his actions," Judge Sok said. "As of now, he is accused of drug possession and smoking in public and will face a judge's order if found guilty."

Story is currently being held at the Battambang provincial prison pending trial. If convicted, he could face up to five years in prison and a fine of up to 1 million riel.

This incident highlights the growing problem of drug abuse and smoking in public in Battambang province. The authorities are urging residents to report any suspicious activity to the police and to avoid engaging in such behavior.

In related news, the Battambang provincial government has announced plans to launch a campaign to raise awareness about the dangers of drug abuse and smoking in public. The campaign, which is set to begin in 2010, will involve community outreach programs, public service announcements, and education on the dangers of drug use.

The authorities are hoping that the campaign will help to reduce the number of drug-related crimes in the province and promote a safer and healthier community for residents.

In the meantime, residents are advised to remain vigilant and to report any suspicious activity to the police. The authorities are committed to making Battambang a safer and healthier place for everyone.
