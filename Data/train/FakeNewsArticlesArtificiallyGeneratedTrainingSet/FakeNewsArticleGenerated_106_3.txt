Despite her tireless work ethic, Caroline's life was turned upside down when her husband was diagnosed with Lewy body dementia. The disease, which is a type of progressive dementia, affects memory, movement, and other cognitive functions, often causing hallucinations and difficulty with physical movements. Caroline's husband, who was only 55 at the time, began experiencing symptoms such as depression, anxiety, and mood swings. 

As the disease progressed, Caroline found herself shouldering more and more of the household responsibilities, often sacrificing her own well-being for the sake of her husband. She started experiencing symptoms of her own, including chronic fatigue, insomnia, and irritability. "It was like I was living in a different world," Caroline said in an interview. "I felt like I was drowning in a sea of uncertainty, with no lifeline to cling to." 

Despite the challenges, Caroline remained committed to her husband's care, and she began to notice subtle changes in his behavior. He would often forget important dates, such as anniversaries and birthdays, and would become easily agitated when trying to accomplish simple tasks. "It was heartbreaking to see my husband, who was once so full of life and energy, struggle to do even the simplest things," Caroline said. 

In an effort to cope with the emotional toll of her husband's disease, Caroline turned to her work as a source of solace. She threw herself into her job, taking on more responsibilities and working long hours to distract herself from the challenges at home. But even her work couldn't shield her from the harsh reality of her husband's condition. 

One day, Trav, Caroline's boss, approached her with a unique proposal. He had heard about a new program that aimed to provide emotional support to caregivers of people with dementia. The program, which was still in its pilot phase, offered free counseling sessions and support groups to help caregivers cope with the emotional strain of their role. Trav suggested that Caroline take advantage of the program, and Caroline, feeling overwhelmed and exhausted, agreed. 

The program turned out to be a lifesaver for Caroline. Through the support group, she met other caregivers who were facing similar challenges, and she began to feel a sense of community and understanding. The counseling sessions helped her to process her emotions and develop coping strategies for the difficult days ahead. 

Thanks to the program, Caroline was able to find a new sense of purpose and meaning in her life. She continued to care for her husband, but she was no longer alone in her struggles.
