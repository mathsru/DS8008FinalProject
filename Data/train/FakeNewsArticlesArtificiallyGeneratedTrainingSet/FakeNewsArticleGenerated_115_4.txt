Battambang was arrested on Wednesday for driving under the influence of drugs after a traffic stop in the state of California. According to eyewitnesses, Battambang was smoking a drug at the time of the arrest, which led to the discovery of the illegal substance.

Battambang, 25, was accused of possessing a controlled substance, a drug that is banned in the state. The judge ordered Battambang to be held without bail pending a hearing scheduled for next week.

The arrest was made by the California Highway Patrol (CHP) at around 11:30 pm on Tuesday, when Battambang was stopped for speeding on the 101 freeway in Ventura County. During the stop, the CHP officer noticed that Battambang was smoking a drug and requested him to step out of the vehicle.

Upon further investigation, the officer found a small amount of the drug in Battambang's possession. Battambang was arrested and taken into custody. The drug was later identified as a controlled substance, which is illegal in the state of California.

The incident is the latest in a series of high-profile arrests involving drugs and driving under the influence in the state. In 2010, Davis Story, a 32-year-old man, was arrested for driving under the influence of drugs and possessing a controlled substance. Story was also accused of smoking a drug at the time of the arrest.

The arrest of Battambang and Story highlights the growing problem of drug use and driving under the influence in the state. The authorities are urging drivers to be aware of the dangers of drug use and to avoid driving under the influence of drugs.

In the meantime, Battambang is being held in custody pending a hearing scheduled for next week. The case is being investigated by the California Highway Patrol and the Ventura County District Attorney's Office. The outcome of the case is still pending and will depend on the evidence presented in court.
