Kim Kardashian was bound, gagged and begging for life during Paris jewelry robbery While tout le monde was buzzing about the shocking, $10 million Paris robbery of Kim Kardashian, French authorities began putting together the pieces of her horrifying ordeal.

As Kardashian put distance between herself and the swanky hotel where she feared for her life Sunday night, details of the holdup, supplied by sources and her reps, began to emerge.

They painted a harrowing picture of a woman who feared she would be killed — or raped — by masked men.

“This was a life and death matter,” a source told the Daily News. “That’s what’s important. The fact people keep focusing on what was stolen is irrelevant. She might have not been on this planet anymore.

“She begged for them to let her live and (said) she has babies at home,” the source said. “Then they wrapped her mouth in tape and put her in the bathtub.”

Citing sources, celebrity gossip site TMZ.com said Kardashian was in bed after a night on the town and wearing a robe when two men stormed the room.

She initially feared they would rape her. She tried to call her bodyguard, but one of the bandits snatched the phone before she could complete the call, according to TMZ.

Kardashian later told cops the men spoke only French, except for one word — ring — a reference to a new, widely publicized bauble she had received from her doting husband, rap star Kanye West.

She told them where it was before they taped her mouth shut. They also bound her ankles with duct tape and zip-tied her hands with plastic handcuffs before putting her in the tub, a source told The News.

Before the robbery, five men threatened a concierge with a weapon and forced him to enter Kardashian’s private two-story residence at the hotel. The suspects made off with a jewelry box, a ring and a pair of cell phones.

Meanwhile, a Kardashian friend who was sleeping in a downstairs bedroom heard the commotion and locked herself in a bathroom.

There, she called Kardashian’s bodyguard, who arrived two minutes after the thieves left.

The entire ordeal lasted six minutes.

One of the men, who were dressed as police officers, even pointed a handgun at Kardashian’s temple before tying her up, according to various accounts.

A rep said the reality star was “badly shaken but physically unharmed.”

The thieves reportedly fled on bicycles, authorities said.

Missing from the chaotic scene was Kardashian’s usually ever-present bodyguard, Pascal Duvier, who was shadowing sisters Kourtney Kardashian and Kendall Jenner at the time, since Kim had turned in for the night, according to TMZ.

Duvier was by Kim Kardashian’s side last week when a notorious prankster ran up and tried to kiss her famous derriere. Duvier tackled the man mid-pucker.

“My security @PascalDuvier is a G,” Kim tweeted after the incident.

Cops also questioned a photographer who had posed as a cop to get a picture of the star in a restaurant earlier in the week, according to The Sun newspaper.

French police force Brigade de Repression du Banditisme, which specializes in armed robbery investigations, is leading the Kardashian heist probe. The elite investigators are known for their ongoing case against the gang known throughout Europe as the “Pink Panthers.”

CBS’s 60 Minutes credited the notorious jewel thieves with over 370 capers worth $500 million.

It’s unclear if the mysterious gang — made up of mostly former soldiers from Yugoslavia — had any part in the Monday theft.

The “Pink Panthers” are known for speed, minimal violence and meticulous planning that utilizes disguises.

The so-called No Address Hotel is a historic private mansion that was converted into luxury apartments with hotel-like accommodations. Among its guests have been Prince, Madonna and Leonardo DiCaprio.
