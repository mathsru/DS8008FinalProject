Early Life

Margot Elise Robbie was born on July 2nd of 1990, in Dalby, Queensland. Raised by a single mother on the Gold Coast alongside three siblings, Margot spent much of her childhood living on the family farm. By the age of 16, Robbie was working three jobs at the same time in order to provide for her family. This included working at Subway. She also managed to juggle her growing passion for acting and studied drama at Somerset College. At the age of 17, Margot moved to Melbourne in order to pursue a career as an actress.

Career

Early Work in Australia

Margot Robbie began her acting career in her native Australia, appearing in low-budget independent films such as "ICU" and "Vigilante" while still a teenager. She quickly gained exposure through national television commercials and landed guest roles on popular Australian shows like "The Elephant Princess" and "City Homicide." Her breakthrough in her home country came in 2008 when she was cast as Donna Freedman on the long-running soap opera "Neighbours." Originally intended to appear in a short-term role, Robbie's charm and talent made her a fan favorite, and she became a series regular. Her work on the show earned her nominations at the Logie Awards and established her as one of Australia's most promising young actresses.

Move to Hollywood and Breakthrough

After leaving "Neighbours" in 2010, Robbie set her sights on Hollywood. She relocated to Los Angeles to audition during pilot season, but despite testing for several network shows, she initially struggled to land a major role. Her persistence paid off in 2013 when she appeared in Richard Curtis's romantic comedy "About Time," a modest success that introduced her to international audiences.

Her true breakout came later that same year with Martin Scorsese's "The Wolf of Wall Street," where she starred opposite Leonardo DiCaprio as Naomi Lapaglia, the glamorous and sharp-tongued wife of DiCaprio's Jordan Belfort. The film was a global hit, earning over $392 million, and Robbie's fearless, scene-stealing performance drew rave reviews. Her convincing Brooklyn accent and magnetic screen presence earned her comparisons to classic Hollywood stars, firmly cementing her as one of the industry's brightest new talents.

Rising Star and Founding LuckyChap Entertainment

In the years that followed, Robbie carefully balanced commercial projects with critically respected work. She co-starred with Will Smith in the slick crime thriller "Focus" (2015) and earned praise for her role in the post-apocalyptic drama "Z for Zachariah" (2015). She also appeared in "Suite Française," "The Big Short," "Whiskey Tango Foxtrot," and "The Legend of Tarzan," showcasing her versatility across genres.

In 2014, Robbie co-founded LuckyChap Entertainment with her husband, Tom Ackerley, and friends Josey McNamara and Sophia Kerr. The company quickly became known for producing female-driven, artistically ambitious films and television projects. Through LuckyChap, Robbie began taking creative control of her career—acting, producing, and championing stories that centered on complex, unconventional women.

Becoming Harley Quinn and Awards Recognition

In 2016, Robbie reached new levels of global fame when she debuted as Harley Quinn in DC's "Suicide Squad." Although the film received mixed reviews, Robbie's charismatic and chaotic portrayal of the beloved comic book antihero was singled out as a standout. Her performance turned Harley Quinn into a cultural phenomenon and made Robbie a household name.

She followed up with roles in "Goodbye Christopher Robin" (2017) and "I, Tonya" (2017), the latter becoming one of her most acclaimed performances to date. As producer and star, Robbie portrayed controversial figure skater Tonya Harding with grit and humanity, earning her first Academy Award nomination for Best Actress, as well as nominations at the Golden Globes, BAFTAs, and Screen Actors Guild Awards. The film also earned a Best Supporting Actress Oscar for Allison Janney, marking a milestone for Robbie's production company.

Established Leading Lady

Robbie continued to build her reputation as one of Hollywood's most bankable and respected talents. She lent her voice to the character Flopsy Rabbit in the animated hit "Peter Rabbit" (2018) and took on dramatic roles in "Terminal" and "Mary Queen of Scots" (2018), where she portrayed Queen Elizabeth I opposite Saoirse Ronan. Her transformation into the aging monarch earned critical acclaim and another BAFTA nomination.

In 2019, she starred in Quentin Tarantino's "Once Upon a Time in Hollywood," playing actress Sharon Tate in a performance that earned praise for its warmth and subtlety. That same year, she appeared in "Bombshell," a dramatization of the Fox News sexual harassment scandal, alongside Charlize Theron and Nicole Kidman. Robbie's portrayal of Kayla Pospisil, a young producer, earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actress.

She began 2020 by reprising her iconic role as Harley Quinn in "Birds of Prey," a female-led superhero film that she also produced. The movie drew praise for Robbie's energy, comedic timing, and commitment to reimagining the character on her own terms.

Recent Work and Ongoing Projects

In the years that followed, Robbie continued to expand her creative reach. She voiced Flopsy again in "Peter Rabbit 2: The Runaway" (2021) and appeared as Harley Quinn once more in James Gunn's "The Suicide Squad" (2021). Through LuckyChap Entertainment, she produced the darkly comedic thriller "Promising Young Woman" (2020), which went on to win the Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay.

Her biggest success came in 2023 with "Barbie," which she both produced and starred in. Directed by Greta Gerwig, the film became a cultural event and one of the highest-grossing movies of all time, surpassing $1.4 billion worldwide. Robbie's dual role—as both the lead performer and creative force behind the project—was widely praised, cementing her status as one of the most influential figures in contemporary Hollywood.

Relationships

In 2014, Margot Robbie began dating British assistant director Tom Ackerley, whom she met on the set of "Suite Française." The couple married in a private ceremony in Byron Bay, Australia, in December 2016. They lived in London for several years before relocating to Los Angeles, where they now run LuckyChap Entertainment together. Despite their growing fame, the couple is known for keeping their relationship private and grounded.
