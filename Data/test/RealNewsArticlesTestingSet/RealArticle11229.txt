Kesha recalls battle with eating disorder Kesha has reflected on the shame she felt during her painful battle with an eating disorder.

The 30-year-old revealed in an interview with Rolling Stone magazine that she had forgotten how to eat - and that the realization is what led her to check into rehab three years ago.

Kesha said it all started while she was attending a dinner party, where her mind was racing as she devised of places to hide her food.

'I was like, "Oh, my God, what if they walk outside and see this food in a bush? Or they see it in the garbage can?" And I just had all this mounting anxiety. And then finally I was like, "F**k. This. Sh*t. F**k this sh*t. I'm hungry!"

'And I am so anxious that I feel like I'm going to explode from all the secrets. All the secret times I'm pretending to eat or other times I'm purging, and I'm trying to not let anybody know.

'And I'm just f**king sick of this sh*t. And I remember just shaking because I was so fed up, so anxious, and I was just mad that I had let myself get to that point.'

Kesha said she asked her mother Pebe Sebert to meet her at a gas station she had later driven to.

'I didn't know how to eat': Kesha said she ended up driving to a gas station, and asking her mother Pebe Sebert to meet her there (pictured August 2017)

'I didn't know how to even eat,' she said. 'At that point, I'd forgotten how to do it.'

Her mother sent her to rehab, where she received education from a nutritionist.

Kesha's inner demons continued to torment her: 'I just remember crying into a carbohydrate, being like, "I can't eat it. It's going to make me fat, and if I'm fat, I can't be a singer because pop stars can't eat food – they can't be fat"'.

During treatment, Kesha's boyfriend Brad Ashenfelter would fly out on the weekends to visit her at rehab, where they would color.

As she continued to recover, a phone call from a friend shifted things into perspective.

Though the friend had just won several Grammy awards, he ended up congratulating Kesha for saving her own life.

'He was like, "Congratulations to you,"' she remembered. 'And I was like, "For what?'" And he was like, "Who cares about my Grammys? You just saved your f**king life." And I just was blown away by that, because it made me look at the whole thing totally differently.'

She said of the realization: 'I did just take my life into my own hands and choose life over a slow, painful, shameful self-imposed death. And I need to stop just being so f**king mean to myself.'

Speaking of the pain she felt during her eating disorder, she explained even consuming food would lead to feelings of shame.

'I don't deserve to eat food': The Tik Tok singer explained she didn't even believe she was supposed to have food, and eating led to feelings of shame (pictured 2017)

'I would make myself throw up because I'd think, "Oh, my God, I can't believe I actually did that horrible thing. I'm so ashamed of myself because I don't deserve to eat food."'

Kesha also explained how, though she was suffering from her eating disorder, she received compliments on her looks.

'I was slowly, slowly starving myself. And the worse I got and the sicker I got, the better a lot of people around me were saying that I looked. They would just be like, "Oh, my gosh, keep doing whatever you're doing! You look so beautiful, so stunning."'

Of course, the singer has also severed ties with producer Dr. Luke, whom she has accused of drugging, raping, and otherwise abusing her.

Emails submitted to the court in her suit against Dr. Luke even outlines ways in which he likely contributed to her eating issuers.

'We all get concerned she is breaking her diet plan…we have seen it happen multiple times…almost every day,' he wrote in June 2012 to Kesha's manager Monica Cornia.

He later elaborated that the diet plan she broke was an all-juice cleanse, during which she had turkey and a Diet Coke.

According to sources who spoke to TMZ, Dr. Luke also told Kesha in 2012 that she 'looks like a f***ing refrigerator'.

Kesha also spoke about her friendship with Taylor Swift in her Rolling Stone interview, raving that the pop star is 'a f**king sweetheart.'

The Shake It Off singer, who donated $250,000 to Kesha to help finance her legal battle against Dr. Luke, is 'very, very sweet, very, very genuine, extremely generous, picks up the phone every time I call her,' Kesha gushed.

The singer also explained she hasn't been keeping up to date on Taylor's pop culture drama: 'Should I know something about it? I live in my record player,' she said.
