Kardashians slam Blac Chyna allegations in new court docs The Kardashian clan has fired back in the ongoing legal feud with Blac Chyna who has accused the family of defaming her and sabotaging her now-canceled reality show Rob & Chyna.

In new court documents obtained by DailyMail.com, Kris Jenner, Rob and Kim Kardashian - who are named as defendants in the suit - have dismissed the reality star's claims as 'bald conclusions that have no factual support.'

Chyna, real name Angela Renee White, first filed the suit in October, blaming the family for cancellation of her reality show, which was not renewed for a second season after 2016.

She claimed E! and production team, as well as herself and Rob, were all on board and ready to shoot a second season before the show was axed.

Chyna also claimed the Kardashians defamed her and have been using their 'fame, wealth, and power' to take her down.'

But, in a reply memorandum filed on January 10, the Kardashians point out that Chyna's own statements affirm that plans for the production the show were never set in stone.

'Plaintiff [Blac Chyna] affirmatively alleges...that on "February 8, 2017, the E! Network sought a seven-month extension" of its option to engage plaintiff's services on a second season of Rob & Chyna, which, according to plaintiff, gave the network until October 16, 2017 to decide whether to pick up a second season of the show,' court papers state.

The network also allegedly paid Chyna for the right to extend its option and even paid her to appear on four episodes of Keeping Up with the Kardashians at the rate she would have been paid for Rob & Chyna.

'These allegations establish that the plaintiff had no contractual right under her 2016 Talent Agreement with E! to actually be engaged for a second season of [the show],' and therefore she could not claim the family interfered, attorneys for the Kardashians said.

The 29-year-old, who is the mother of Rob's daughter Dream, also accused the family of defamation after they alleged she left her baby home alone while she went to a party.

The family argued that they did in fact say she left her daughter at home, but said Chyna left the baby with the nanny.

Chyna also accused the family of libel while referencing a story allegedly posted by TMZ.

'That paragraph, however, does not identify any allegedly false written statements made by the defendants,' the court papers read.

Attorneys for the family also noted that Chyna's last filing was her 'second attempt to plead viable causes of action' but has failed to fix them.

They also requested for her complaint to be sustained and not amended.

The court filing comes weeks after the former stripper accused the family of throwing their weight around to hamper her career after her split with Rob.

'The unwritten rule no one told [her] when she began her relationship with Kardashian is that the entire family will come after you if you leave,' Chyna said in an earlier court filing.

The salon owner said that the Kardashians have no hesitation 'about using their fame, wealth and power to take you down, including getting your television show cancelled... and spreading lies about you' if you fall on their bad side.

Chyna said that the reason they were aggressive in their attacks on her was to curtail her success in fields they compete in, such as social media and products like cosmetics.

Chyna said that the family funded 'a bogus lawsuit about a crumbled gingerbread house,' referencing a lawsuit Rob filed last fall accusing Chyna of assault, battery and vandalism, according to TMZ.

In a recent episode of Keeping Up With The Kardashians, Kim addressed the feud between her brother and Blac Chyna

Rob claimed that she had destroyed a gingerbread house during a December 2016 meltdown in which she hit him in the face, attempted to choke him with an iPhone cord, and amassed more than $100,000 in damage to a property he'd rented from Kylie Jenner in tearing up the walls and a door, and destroying a TV.

In that suit, Rob said that the incident was the real reason E! cancelled their show, saying that the circumstances were too mercurial.

Lawyers for the Kardashians have said the family is not responsible for Rob & Chyna's cancellation, saying that production could not continue after Chyna obtained a restraining order against Rob last summer.
