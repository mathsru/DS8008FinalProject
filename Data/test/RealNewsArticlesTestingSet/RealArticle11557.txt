Black Golden Globes Outfits to Be Auctioned Off for the Time's Up Legal Defense Fund Earlier this month, stars including Reese Witherspoon, Tracee Ellis Ross, Mandy Moore, Nicole Kidman, and Laura Dern wore all black to the 2018 Golden Globes in a show of solidarity with survivors of sexual harassment and assault, as well as to raise awareness about anti-workplace harassment initiative Time's Up. Now, they're putting those red carpet outfits up for auction to benefit the movement's legal defense fund. The initiative was announced Wednesday by Time's Up, eBay, and Condé Nast—Glamour's parent company—who are teaming up to run the auction taking place this Friday.

Tracee Ellis Ross in Marc Jacobs

"We’re thrilled that actors and others in the entertainment industry are leading in this extraordinary way to end the scourge of workplace sexual harassment,” said Fatima Goss Graves, the President and CEO of the National Women’s Law Center, which is in charge of Time's Up's legal defense fund. “Each time they’ve stepped up they’ve inspired more attorneys to join the Time's Up Legal Defense Fund and more individuals to contribute."

The black dresses and tuxedos were donated by the designers, who include Versace, Vera Wang, Armani, Balenciaga, Gucci, Prada, and many others, and all money raised will be donated to the Time's Up legal defense fund. The outfits will be available for bidding starting January 19, here, with the auction running through January 26.
