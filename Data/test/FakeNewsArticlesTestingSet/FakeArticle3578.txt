Corey Gamble Net Worth What is Corey Gamble's Net Worth?

Corey Gamble is an American talent manager and television personality who has a net worth of $15 million. Corey Gamble is primarily known as the long-term boyfriend of Kris Jenner, the matriarch of the Kardashian-Jenner family. Gamble began dating Jenner in 2014 after meeting at designer Riccardo Tisci's 40th birthday party in Ibiza.

Professionally, he works in the entertainment industry as a talent manager and is affiliated with Scooter Braun's SB Projects, where he has helped manage Justin Bieber's career, among others.

Before entering the entertainment world, he studied business marketing at Morehouse College. Gamble has become a regular presence on the family's reality shows, including "Keeping Up with the Kardashians" and "The Kardashians," where he's known for his calm demeanor and supportive relationship with Jenner. Despite their 25-year age gap, he has integrated well into the Kardashian-Jenner family dynamic, often serving as a voice of reason during family conflicts.

Early Life

Corey Gamble was born on November 8, 1980, in Atlanta, Georgia. He attended Westlake High School, where he was a member of the football team, and his friends called him "Mr. Dependable" due to his ability to balance his school work and athletic activities. After graduating in 1999, Corey enrolled at Morehouse College, and he graduated with a degree in Business Management.

Career

After graduating from college, Gamble moved to Los Angeles, California, to try to make a name in the music business. He began working with Scooter Braun, Justin Bieber's manager, who was named one of "Time" magazine's 100 Most Influential People in the World in 2013. Corey is the tour manager for Braun's entertainment and marketing company SB Projects, which has represented music stars such as Ariana Grande, Idina Menzel, and Demi Lovato. Since he started dating Kris Jenner, Corey has appeared on her E! reality series, "Keeping Up with the Kardashians," several times, and he appeared on two episodes of Bravo's "The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills" in 2020.

Jenner is the mother of Kim, Kourtney, Khloé, and Robert Kardashian, and Kylie and Kendall Jenner.
